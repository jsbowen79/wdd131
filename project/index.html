Budgeting

What is budgeting?

Put simply, budgeting is dividing your income into chunks reserved for a particular purpose.

Click here to learn more about Budgets

What are the benefits of budgeting?

  • Gain Power over your money

    Budgets show where your money is going and allow you to make more informed decisions about your spending.

  • Planning for the Future

    Budgeting allows you to control how much money you spend on individual expenses which allows you to reserve money for specific purposes such as retirement, paying off debt, purchasing a home, or establishing an emergency fund.

  • Manage Spending

    Budgets highlight "needs" versus "wants" and help you to control wasteful and discretionary spending.

  • Financial Stability

    Knowing how much money you have and how much you can spend helps prevent overspending and provides financial stability.

How do I start budgeting?

It's Easy!

  1. List all of your income sources in one column
  2. List all of your bills in another column
  3. Total each column separately
  4. Calculate: Total income - Total Bills = Net Income
  5. Divide Net Income into appropriate groups
  6. Keep spending within allotted amounts for each category
  7. Done!
